• Step 1: Choose any three stars sufficiently close to the zenith. (Their zenith distance must be greater than {80^\circ}.) You can turn on the zenith circles using the key 'Z' if you wish.
  • Step 2: Note down the altitudes, a, of these three stars. 90-a will give us their zenithal distances, z_1, z_2, and z_3
  • Step 3: Write down the RA and declination of these three stars.
  • Step 4: Read and note the date and time.
  • Step 5: Use the information from Steps 3 and 4 to determine the sub stellar point of the three stars.
  • Step 6: Get a map of a certain region of the earth. All three of your substellar points should be on this map.
  • Step 7: Mark off all three substellar points on the map.
  • Step 7: Draw a circle with the centre at the substellar point, and radius equal to the zenithal distance of that particular star. Do this for all three stars
  • Step 8: Ideally all the circles should intersect at exactly one point. This point is the observer location. Read off its co-ordinates.
  • Step 9: If the circles do not intersect exactly, then read off the centroid of the three points of intersection. This is the observer location.
